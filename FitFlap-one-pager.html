FitFlap makes your body the controller. Strap a phone to your arm, raise and lower it, and pilot a bird through obstacles in real time — the workout is the game, not a tracker you check afterward. Our hackathon demo, Flappy Workout, runs entirely in the browser with an iPhone as the controller and a laptop as the screen.

The Problem

People quit workouts because they feel repetitive and lonely. Fitness apps track after you sweat — they don't make the set itself fun. VR fitness does, but it's expensive, bulky, and overkill for a quick daily burn.

How It Works

1
Open on a laptop. The game shows a QR code — no app, no install.
2
Scan with your iPhone. It pairs over a WebSocket relay and becomes your motion controller.
3
Calibrate in 2 taps (arm low / high). Now your arm drives the bird's height live.
4
One up-and-down curl = 1 rep. Thread the gaps, bank a score, climb the leaderboard.

Why It's Different

  • The workout is the game — engagement happens during the set, not in a post-hoc dashboard.
  • No headset, no console, no install — just the phone already on your arm and any screen.
  • A platform, not a game — one motion controller spanning a library of workouts. Flappy Workout is Game 01.

What We Shipped — Live Demo

  • ✓iPhone ↔ laptop, paired by QR over a WebSocket relay — 100% browser, zero install.
  • ✓Drift-free, mount-agnostic motion — arm height from a gravity-vector sweep; works however the phone is strapped.
  • ✓Real rep detection via hysteresis on a full up→down cycle.
  • ✓Difficulty that scales the workout — gaps zig-zag and speed ramps, forcing wider arm sweeps.
  • ✓Volume tracking — reps × weight = total lb / kg lifted.
  • ✓Live shared leaderboard + a mouse fallback for instant demos.
  • ✓One-command deploy to Google Cloud Run (auto-HTTPS, which is what unlocks iOS motion).
